A web-based reservation system designed to simplify the appointment booking process for patients while reducing administrative workload for clinic staff through an intuitive, self-service platform.

The Problem: Understanding the User

Current clinic booking systems create friction for both patients and staff, leading to phone congestion, scheduling errors, and administrative bottlenecks that impact healthcare access.

User Personas

The Anxious Patient

"I dread calling to book appointments. The hold times are long and I always feel rushed."

  • Age: 68, retired teacher
  • Limited tech comfort but willing to learn
  • Values clear communication
  • Prefers self-service to avoid phone anxiety

The Efficient Admin

"I spend 70% of my day on the phone booking appointments instead of helping patients in person."

  • Age: 42, clinic administrator
  • Tech-savvy but time-constrained
  • Needs to minimize errors
  • Wants to focus on complex cases

Problem Statement

"Patients and clinic staff need a streamlined, self-service appointment booking system because the current phone-based process is time-consuming, prone to errors, and creates administrative bottlenecks that reduce efficiency and patient satisfaction."

Key Learnings

This project taught me the critical importance of designing for diverse technical comfort levels. Initially, I assumed a standard date picker would be sufficient, but testing revealed that older adults—a significant portion of clinic patients—found it confusing. This led me to implement a more explicit, text-based date selection option alongside the traditional calendar.

I also learned that in healthcare contexts, clarity and reassurance are paramount. Adding prominent confirmation numbers, clear next steps, and the option to print or save appointment details significantly increased user confidence in the system.

The experience reinforced that even seemingly minor interface decisions can have substantial impacts on user trust and adoption rates, especially in domains where accuracy is critical.
